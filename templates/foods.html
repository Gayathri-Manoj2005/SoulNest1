Dog Food Options

Choose recipes based on age, activity level, and any special sensitivities.

Chicken & Rice Complete

Dry kibble · Adult dogs

  • Balanced protein, carbs and healthy fats.
  • Fortified with omega-3 & 6 for skin and coat.
  • Ideal for moderately active indoor dogs.

Best for: Labs, Indies, Beagles

Gentle Tummy Lamb & Pumpkin

Dry kibble · Sensitive stomach

  • Easily digestible lamb as single protein source.
  • Pumpkin and rice for gut support.
  • No wheat, soy or artificial colours.

Best for: Dogs with loose stool / vomiting issues

Slow-Cooked Chicken Stew

Wet food · Picky eaters

  • High moisture to keep dogs hydrated.
  • Real shredded chicken and veggies.
  • Can be mixed with kibble as a topper.

Great as: Weekend treat or topper

Suggested Treats & Snacks

Dental Chew Sticks

Helps reduce tartar build-up and keeps breath fresher. Look for vet-approved dental chews.

Soft Training Treats

Small, low-calorie treats ideal for clicker training and rewarding good behaviour.

Dehydrated Meat Jerky

Single-ingredient chicken or fish jerky with no added salt or sugar is a high-value reward.

Snacks should be at most about 10% of your dog’s daily calories.

Cat Food Options

Focus on high-protein recipes with taurine and controlled minerals.

Indoor Hairball Control

Dry food · Adult indoor cats

  • High fibre blend to minimise hairballs.
  • Controlled calories to prevent weight gain.
  • Added taurine for heart and eye health.

Best for: Long-hair & indoor-only cats

Kitten Growth Formula

Dry & wet · 2–12 month kittens

  • High protein and DHA for brain development.
  • Smaller kibble size for tiny mouths.
  • Supports strong bones and immunity.

Feed until: 1 year of age

Urinary Care Gravy Pouches

Wet food · Neutered adults

  • Helps maintain healthy urinary pH.
  • Encourages water intake through gravy.
  • Great for cats that avoid drinking water.

Vet-recommended for urinary-prone cats

Suggested Treats & Snacks

Crunchy Dental Bits

Helps control plaque when used along with brushing and regular vet cleanings.

Lickable Purée Treats

Perfect for bonding, hiding medicine, or tempting shy cats to eat.

Freeze-Dried Meat Cubes

Single-protein treats like chicken or salmon with no grains or fillers.

Cat treats are calorie-dense — keep them to just a few pieces a day.

Rabbit Food Options

Rabbits need unlimited hay plus a small portion of pellets daily.

Timothy Hay Premium

Long-strand hay · Daily base diet

  • Helps wear down teeth naturally.
  • High fibre for healthy digestion.
  • Offer in unlimited quantity.

Suitable for: Adult rabbits

Alfalfa Growth Mix

Hay & pellet blend · Young rabbits

  • Higher calcium and protein for growth.
  • Feed to rabbits under 6–7 months.
  • Transition to timothy hay as they mature.

Use for juveniles or pregnant does only

Natural Veggie Treat Bites

Treat · 2–3 pieces per day

  • Made from dried carrot, herbs and hay.
  • No added sugar, colours or seeds.
  • Use for bonding and training.

Treats < 5% of daily diet

Suggested Treats & Snacks

Dried Herb Mix

A pinch of dried mint, chamomile or parsley as an occasional fragrant treat.

Compressed Hay Cubes

Fun to toss and chew while still being mostly timothy hay and high in fibre.

Small Fresh Veg Pieces

Very small portions of carrot, coriander or leafy greens, introduced slowly.

Avoid sugary snacks; rabbit treats should always be hay-based or veggie-based.

Bird Food Options

A good bird diet combines quality seed, pellets and fresh veggies.

Fortified Seed Mix

Seeds · Budgies & cockatiels

  • Sunflower-controlled formula to avoid obesity.
  • Includes millet, canary seed and oats.
  • Enriched with vitamins and minerals.

Serve with fresh greens daily

Complete Pellet Diet

Pellets · Parrots & larger birds

  • Every pellet contains balanced nutrition.
  • Prevents selective eating of only favourite seeds.
  • Available in different sizes and flavours.

Introduce gradually mixed with old food

Calcium & Mineral Blocks

Supplement · All pet birds

  • Supports bone strength and laying hens.
  • Helps trim beaks naturally.
  • Keep attached to perch or cage bars.

Always offer alongside fresh water

Suggested Treats & Snacks

Millet Sprays

A favourite for budgies and cockatiels — hang a short piece as a special reward.

Natural Nut Pieces

Unsalted almond or walnut pieces for parrots, given sparingly due to high fat.

Dried Fruit Bits (No Sugar)

Tiny pieces of unsweetened apple or papaya tucked into foraging toys.

Keep bird treats small and occasional so they don’t crowd out pellets, seeds and fresh veggies.
